                    <td valign="top" class="mcnTextContent" style="padding: 0px 18px 9px;line-height: 150%;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;word-break: break-word;color: var(--body-color);font-family: 'Lora', Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;">

                      <p style="line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--body-color);font-family: 'Lora', Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;">
                        <strong>Weighing neutrinos</strong>. Neutrinos are elusive, weakly interacting particles that are produced in vast numbers by nuclear reactions. Every second, <a href="https://ase.tufts.edu/cosmos/view_chapter.asp?id=37" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">roughly 100 billion solar neutrinos
                          pass through the tip of your finger</a>, even at night, when they must pass through the Earth to reach you. Neutrino detectors are used to <a href="https://phys.org/news/2018-10-snapshot-spectrum-neutrinos-emitted-sun.html" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">understand the sun</a>, <a href="https://www.vox.com/science-and-health/2018/7/12/17552586/neutrino-cosmic-ray-ice-cube-multimessenger-astronomy-astrophysics-nsf" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">study the most powerful cosmic ray
                          accelerators</a>, and have been proposed for <a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/srep33034" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">imaging the inside of the Earth</a>
                        and <a href="https://www.wired.com/2016/09/quest-build-portable-anti-neutrino-detector-nukes/" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">searching for illegal nuclear
                          reactors</a>. In the 1990s, physicists discovered that <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neutrino_oscillation" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">neutrinos oscillate between three
                          types</a>, or "flavors", and therefore that <a href="https://theconversation.com/physics-duo-wins-the-nobel-prize-for-solving-longstanding-neutrino-puzzle-48702" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">they must have mass</a>. (Basically:
                        if they oscillate, they're experiencing time, and if they experience time, they cannot move at the speed of light and this must be because they have mass. QED.) Since neutrinos have mass, a stream of neutrinos will tend to
                        “drag a little bit of matter” along with them, resulting in a <a href="https://theconversation.com/ghost-particles-how-galaxies-helped-us-weigh-the-lightest-neutrino-and-why-it-matters-122230" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">“fuzzing” of the distribution of
                          galaxies</a> and affecting the development of large-scale galactic structure (<a href="https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.45.1980" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">paper</a>). A <a href="https://www.ucl.ac.uk/news/2019/aug/maximum-mass-lightest-neutrino-revealed-using-astronomical-big-data" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">new study</a> (<a href="https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.123.081301" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">paper</a>) used observations of this
                        effect on 1.1 million galaxies to theorize a lower bound on the mass of the lightest neutrino flavor at 0.086 electron volts (eV) — that’s 6 million times lighter than the mass of an electron. Soon, the <a href="https://www.desi.lbl.gov/" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument</a> (DESI) will observe 10x as many galaxies and be able to improve these bounds. (Another recent <a href="https://physics.aps.org/articles/v12/129" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">paper</a> found an upper limit of 1
                        eV using the beta decay of tritium.) Understanding neutrinos may answer a fundamental question about our existence: why is there any matter in the Universe at all? Cosmology predicts that the <a href="https://home.cern/science/physics/matter-antimatter-asymmetry-problem" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">Big Bang should have created exactly
                          the same amount of matter and antimatter</a>, resulting in mutual annihilation, but our existence suggests otherwise. It’s <a href="https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2017/08/170804083109.htm" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">suspected that a small difference in
                          the behavior of neutrinos and antineutrinos during their oscillations may have resulted in a tiny bit more matter being created</a>, which we’re pretty thankful for.</p>

In it, the agency hypes Artemis 1 and SLS, but noticeably only commits to a <a href="https://www.nasa.gov/exploration/systems/sls/multimedia/what-is-green-run-infographic.html" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">“green run” test firing</a> and not

                    <td valign="top" class="mcnTextContent" style="padding: 0px 18px 9px;line-height: 150%;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;word-break: break-word;color: var(--body-color);font-family: 'Lora', Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;">

                      <p style="line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--body-color);font-family: 'Lora', Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;">
                        <strong>TESS found its first Earth-sized exoplanet in a habitable zone. </strong>The planet, <a href="https://exoplanets.nasa.gov/news/1617/nasa-planet-hunter-finds-earth-size-habitable-zone-world/" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">TOI 700 d, as it is lovingly
                          referred to, is approximately100 light-years away by NASA's Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS)</a> using the <a href="https://www.planetary.org/explore/space-topics/exoplanets/transit-photometry.html" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">transit method</a> and was confirmed
                        by the <a href="https://www.nasa.gov/mission_pages/spitzer/main/index.html" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">Spitzer Space Telescope</a>. The
                        planet is 20% larger than Earth, likely <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tidal_locking" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">tidally locked</a>, and receives
                        ~86% of the stellar energy (‘<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_irradiance" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">insolation</a>’) that our planet
                        does. (For comparison, Mars receives only 43%, while Venus gets 191% that of Earth.) Initially, the host star was incorrectly categorized as a larger star, leading to its exoplanets being assumed to be proportionally larger as
                        well—the error was eventually found by a team that included a high school student, and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TOI_700" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">TOI  700</a> was reclassified as a
                        smaller, dimmer <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stellar_classification" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">M-class star</a> leading to the
                        discovery (<a href="https://youtu.be/QU0qsIGS6MQ" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: var(--mc-link-color);font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;" target="_blank">video</a>). This planet joins a small handful of Earth-size exoplanets found habitable zones by other missions.</p>
